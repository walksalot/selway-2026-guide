      04Packing

      The golden rule: no cotton

      Synthetics, wool, and fleece only — cotton is dangerous when wet. Pack light: everything goes in the dry bags SOAR gives you at orientation — one large + one small per person.

      You bring

      • Quick-dry layers + 1–2 warm fleece/wool layers
      • Long underwear · warm hat & gloves
      • Rain jacket
      • Water shoes + camp shoes (closed-toe is smart — rattlesnakes) + hiking shoes
      • Wool or neoprene socks
      • Sun hat · sunglasses with a cord · sunblock
      • Bug spray — picaridin only (see below)
      • Headlamp · water bottle · toiletries · your meds — doubled, split between the two bags
      • Small camp pillow · day backpack
      • Your rod (4–5 wt, 4-piece, in a case) — or take a free loaner:
        Tap a loaner

      SOAR provides — don't pack

      • Tent, sleeping bag & sleeping pad
      • Dry bags (one large + one small, issued at orientation)
      • Life jacket · paddle/splash gear
      • All food — every meal, riverside

      Seriously — leave the camping gear at home. The outfit carries it all.

      No DEET, ever. DEET dissolves fly lines and technical fabrics. Bring picaridin bug spray instead (Sawyer makes it).

      Fishing rules of the trip: July is hopper season. Catch-and-release only; pinch your barbs — that's our group standard. No waders needed (warm wet-wading) and no spiked or cleated boots — rubber soles only, Ari's rule for the boats. Bringing your own fly rod? 4–5 weight, 4-piece, packed in a case for travel. Bringing a spinning rod instead? Also fine — 4–6 lb line, and lures are covered.

      06Good to know

      No cell service for 6 days. The guides carry Garmin inReach satellite messengers (nightly check-ins), and Steve is bringing a Starlink Mini for emergencies and quick check-ins home.

      Whitewater is Class III–IV. No experience needed, but be in reasonable shape — you'll carry your own gear short distances. It's a blast.

      Allergies: if you have any sting or food allergy, bring two epinephrine auto-injectors and tell Kris + the guides at orientation.
